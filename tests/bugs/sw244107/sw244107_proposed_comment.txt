h3. Conclusion

With ACL {{rule 1 deny}}, *the egress ACL match counter continues to increment at the full offered rate (~2.29–2.40 Mpps) across all four phases* — baseline, uBFD-configured, BGP-single-hop-BFD-configured, and post-cleanup.

# The egress ACL is *enforcing the deny* (no frames leave bundle-10 beyond protocol traffic) and *simultaneously counting the matches*.
# Adding uBFD or BGP SH-BFD to bundle-10 / to a BGP neighbor on the same path *does not disable, reset, or slow the egress ACL counter*.
# This is consistent with the previous run's observation (ACL=allow): *egress ACL and SH-BFD/uBFD coexist without functional impact on this device / software* — the "expected non-support" the user-story describes cannot be reproduced here.

*Caveat:* BFD session did not come Up in either run because the remote side is not a BFD speaker. A follow-up with an actual BFD peer (e.g. Spirent EmulatedDevice BFD or a second DNOS) would confirm behaviour under an *active* BFD session; that case remains uncovered.
